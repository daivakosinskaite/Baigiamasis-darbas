Current Trending Travel Destinations


Journeys to Distant Shores


Southeast Asia

Traditionally popular destinations in this region include Bali, Sri Lanka, and Thailand. Convenient transportation, affordable prices, have been attracting tourists from all over the world for years, so the trends will remain unchanged this year. The most popular destinations will continue to be Thailand, Bali, Sri Lanka, Maldives. Accommodation services range from 5-star hotels to very cheap ones, but not always with comfort. However, the heart of Asian tourism is known for its friendly prices for accommodation, food, and entertainment. Of course, if you choose the Maldives, you need to think more carefully about the expenses and assess them more accurately.

South and North America

Longing for more exoticism, people will choose Mexico, Australia, Hawaii. These lands delight with their views, flavors, and perfect environment for coding, hacking, and other pleasant activities.
Of course, these destinations will require larger financial investments, but for programmers, this is not a problem. Hawaii is rightfully called paradise on earth. Mexico and other Central American countries charm with their exoticism and friendly locals, but here, safety issues should be evaluated, and the best choice is to travel with travel agencies.

Africa

Africa is closer to us in terms of distance, but culturally it is completely exotic, if we exclude more frequently visited countries by Lithuanians - Egypt, Morocco, Tunisia, where many have already been. But Kenya, Tanzania, or Namibia are little-known and very interesting countries. Here, there is not only unique nature with well-known savannah inhabitants or tribal people, but also friendly people, and a fairly safe environment. These countries are quite economically strong and take care of the safety of their residents and tourists.

Mountain Journeys for Programmers in 2024


To take a break from programming (although who wants to rest from it?), you can go to snowy mountains to ski, hike, or simply soak in a hot tub while enjoying the views.

The Alps

The Alps traditionally attract Lithuanians because it's easy to reach them by car - the Austrian Alps can be reached in just one day. They are very popular for both summer hikes and winter fun. There are plenty of ski resorts here. And if skiing is scary or not desired, you can take a cable car to the peaks and simply enjoy a cup of coffee while looking through the café windows. Snow-covered mountains never leave anyone indifferent. Skiing Guide

Mountains of Central Asia

The mountains of Central and Far Asia - greater exoticism, offering unexperienced impressions. After all, here lies the highest peak of our planet - Everest. So, this direction is more suitable for amateur climbers. Less experienced travelers can test their strength in smaller mountains, and often they can simply enjoy the hospitality of the residents of Kazakh yurts.

Mountains of South and North America

The mountains of South and North America - another interesting and distant direction, rewarding not only with the best impressions but also with the opportunity to feel real cultural differences. By the way, in North America, there are also the world's longest hiking trails - the Pacific Crest Trail, stretching for 4300 km and passing through various climate zones. Travelers walk it for about half a year, but only tens of them complete it.




Can Programmers Find Lithuania Interesting?


In Lithuania, one can perfectly escape from civilization and enjoy tranquility.

Kibin Capital - Trakai

Trakai is one of the most beautiful towns in Lithuania, attracting crowds of tourists. If you like hustle and bustle, kibinai, and history - Trakai is just for you. There are plenty of dining establishments, pedestrian and cycling paths, and you can also take boat or canoe trips. Accommodation facilities eagerly await guests. In Trakai, you can spend a perfect lunchtime, or you can plan a week of active or passive relaxation.

Forests and National Parks

Labanoras Forest and other wooded areas in Lithuania are a real find for travelers from Western and Southern Europe, where forests have survived less. And for Lithuanians themselves, they usually evoke childhood memories and summers spent at their grandparents' house. National, regional parks, forests, etc. - here are full of hiking and cycling trails, you can pick berries, mushrooms, or relax in rural tourism homesteads listening to bird songs or enjoying Lithuanian sauna pleasures.

Nida

Nida - a pearl of Lithuanian nature, famous all over the world. The unique landscape of dunes is more reminiscent of the Sahara than Lithuania. It is a perfect place to recharge and gain new impressions. Nida is loved by enthusiasts of more peaceful relaxation, there is less noise, fewer guests, cars, so you can focus on relaxation, being with yourself or loved ones, meditation, self-discovery, or simply programming. Important to know before traveling:

  • Plan your trip in advance to avoid unforeseen situations.
  • Double-check your travel documents and reservations before departure.
    1. Passport: Essential for international travel.
    2. Visa(s): If required for entry into your destination country.
    3. Boarding Pass: For access to your flight or other modes of transport.
    4. Itinerary: Details of your travel plans, including flight/train/bus schedules.
    5. Hotel Reservations: Confirmation of your accommodation bookings.
    6. Travel Insurance: Policy documents, if applicable.
    7. Identification: Other forms of ID besides your passport may be needed, such as a driver's license or national ID card.
    8. Vaccination Records: Proof of certain vaccinations may be required.
    9. Credit Cards/Cash: Means of payment for expenses during your trip.
    10. Emergency Contacts: Important numbers or contacts for assistance during your travels.
  • Pack essential items and medications in your luggage.
  • Familiarize yourself with local cultural customs and rule differences.
  • Keep your valuables and money in secure places.
  • Remember safety and health tips during your journey.
  • Enjoy local food and experiences, but be cautious with street food.
  • Have flexible plans to adapt to unpredictable situations.
  • Engage with local residents and learn more about their culture.
  • Embrace adventure but maintain responsibility and respect for others."
